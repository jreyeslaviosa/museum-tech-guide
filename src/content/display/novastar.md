Novastar is a leading manufacturer of LED display control systems — specifically the sending cards, receiving cards, and video processors that drive LED panel video walls. Where pixel controllers (Advatek, DMXking) deal in ArtNet and lighting protocols, Novastar works at the video level: it takes an HDMI or DVI signal and distributes it across a tiled array of LED panel modules.

## How It Works

An LED video wall needs two layers of hardware:
1. **Sending card / video processor** (Novastar MCTRL, VX series) — receives the video signal from a computer or media server and maps it across the panel layout
2. **Receiving card** (built into each panel module) — decodes the signal and drives the physical LEDs in that tile

Novastar's software (NovaLCT, VNNOX) handles calibration, brightness uniformity correction, and remote monitoring.

## Key Products

- **MCTRL 4K:** 4K sending card for large-scale walls; supports up to 4×1080p or 1×4K input
- **VX1000:** All-in-one video controller with built-in sending card and seamless switching
- **H series receiving cards:** Standard receiving cards embedded in most compatible panel modules

## Alternatives

- **Brompton Technology:** Premium processor brand; known for Tessera system used in high-end broadcast and virtual production (XR stages)
- **Linsn:** Budget-friendly alternative; widely used in rental LED panels
- **Colorlight:** Mid-range; popular in permanent installation panels

## Use Cases

- Driving permanent LED video wall installations in galleries and lobbies
- Connecting a media server (Watchout, disguise) to an LED panel array
- Calibrating brightness and colour uniformity across a multi-panel wall
- Remote monitoring and fault detection in unmanned gallery installations
