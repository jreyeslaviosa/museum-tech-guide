Projectors are the output hardware for any projection-based installation. Choosing the right projector involves understanding throw ratio, brightness (lumens), resolution, light source type, and the physical constraints of the space. The wrong choice is expensive and hard to fix post-install.

## Key Specifications

**Brightness (lumens / ANSI lumens)**
The most critical spec. A rule of thumb: for every foot of projection distance, you need roughly 500–800 lumens in a controlled dark space. Ambient light multiplies the requirement significantly.

**Throw Ratio**
The ratio of projection distance to image width. A 1.5:1 throw ratio projects a 3m wide image from 4.5m. Short-throw and ultra-short-throw (UST) projectors allow large images in confined spaces.

**Light Source**
- **Laser:** Long life (20,000+ hours), instant on/off, consistent colour over time, no lamp replacement. The standard for permanent installations
- **Lamp (UHP):** Cheaper upfront, degrades over 2,000–4,000 hours, needs warm-up. Avoid for permanent installs

**Resolution:** 1080p, WUXGA (1920×1200), 4K, and higher. Match to content and viewing distance.

## Key Manufacturers

- **Christie:** Premium, widely used in large-scale installations and theme parks
- **Barco:** Professional/broadcast grade; strong in simulation and events
- **Epson:** EB-L series laser projectors offer excellent value for museum use
- **Sony:** High-end 4K laser; used in premium permanent installations
- **Panasonic:** PT series; strong in permanent install and rental markets

## Use Cases

- Single or multi-projector blended panoramic installations
- Projection mapping onto sculptural objects and architectural surfaces
- Rear-projection screens in exhibition displays
- Short-throw interactive projection on floors or tables
