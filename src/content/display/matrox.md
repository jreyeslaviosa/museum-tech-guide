Matrox is a Canadian graphics hardware company specialising in multi-display workstation graphics cards and video wall controllers. Their products are a common choice in professional AV, broadcast, and simulation environments where a single workstation needs to drive many displays simultaneously with stability and precision.

## Key Product Lines

**C-Series Graphics Cards**
Matrox C-series cards (C680, C420) are workstation graphics cards with 6 or 4 miniDisplayPort outputs from a single PCIe slot. Designed for reliability in 24/7 operation rather than GPU rendering performance — ideal for a workstation that needs to drive 4–6 displays continuously without gaming-class GPU overhead.

**Mura Series — Video Wall Controllers**
Dedicated video wall controller hardware (Mura MPX, Mura IPX) that manages large display arrays independently of the workstation OS. The IPX series uses IP network inputs, making it suitable for large distributed display installations.

**Extio Series — Remote Workstation Extenders**
KVM-over-IP devices that extend a workstation's displays, keyboard, and mouse to a remote location over Cat6 or fibre. Relevant for installations where the PC must be in a machine room but the displays are in a gallery space.

## Matrox vs Datapath

Both make multi-output display cards, with significant overlap. Matrox C-series cards are generally preferred for high output count (6 displays per card) and long-term stability; Datapath FX4 is preferred for video wall-specific features like pixel-perfect bezel compensation and their WallControl software.

## Use Cases

- Driving 4–6 displays from a single workstation in a gallery or control room
- Large video wall installations using Mura controllers with IP video sources
- Machine-room workstations with displays extended to gallery spaces via Extio
- 24/7 permanent installations requiring certified, stable multi-display hardware
