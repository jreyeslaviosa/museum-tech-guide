HDBaseT is a connectivity standard that transmits uncompressed 4K video, audio, 100Mb Ethernet, control signals (RS-232, IR), and power (PoE) over a single Cat5e or Cat6 cable up to 100 metres. It is the standard solution for long-distance signal extension in permanent AV installations — replacing the rats' nest of separate HDMI, network, and power cables with one structured cable run.

## Why It Matters

HDMI cables have a practical limit of around 5–10 metres before signal degradation. In a museum gallery, the display is rarely that close to the source device. HDBaseT extenders (a transmitter at the source, a receiver at the display) solve this cleanly using the building's existing Cat6 infrastructure.

## The 5Play Feature Set

HDBaseT's "5Play" carries five signal types simultaneously over one cable:
1. **Uncompressed video** — up to 4K/30fps or 1080p/60fps depending on spec
2. **Audio** — multi-channel PCM
3. **100BaseT Ethernet** — full network connectivity to the display end
4. **Control** — RS-232 serial and IR pass-through for display control
5. **Power** — up to 100W PoE to power the receiver (and sometimes the display)

## Key Manufacturers

- **Wyrestorm:** Good range of extenders, switchers, and matrix routers
- **Atlona:** Professional-grade; strong in education and corporate AV
- **Kramer:** Wide range including long-distance fibre variants
- **Extron:** Premium; widely specified in permanent installations

## Use Cases

- Extending a media server output to a display 30–80 metres away through a building's structured cabling
- Running a single Cat6 to each gallery display, carrying video, network, and power
- Replacing long HDMI runs in existing installations without re-cabling
- Matrix switching — route any of several sources to any of several displays over Cat6
