BrightSign makes dedicated solid-state media player hardware specifically designed for digital signage, kiosks, and unattended display installations. They are the most common media player found in museum interactives and permanent gallery displays — built for years of continuous operation without maintenance.

## Why BrightSign over a PC or Raspberry Pi

BrightSign players run a locked-down OS optimised purely for media playback. They boot in seconds, never need software updates mid-installation, tolerate extreme temperatures, and are designed to run 24/7 for years. For a simple looping video display in a gallery, they are more reliable and cheaper to operate than a full PC.

## Product Range

- **LS series:** Entry-level; single full-HD output; simple looping video
- **HD series:** Mid-range; supports touch, HTML5 interactives, and basic networking
- **XD series:** Multi-zone; dual outputs; live streaming input
- **XT series:** 4K; advanced networking; HTML5 and JavaScript applications
- **XC series:** Compact form factor for tight installations

## Use Cases

- Looping video and audio in a gallery display case or kiosk enclosure
- Running an HTML5 interactive exhibit without a full desktop PC
- Digital wayfinding and information displays throughout a museum
- Any unattended display that must run reliably for years without IT intervention
