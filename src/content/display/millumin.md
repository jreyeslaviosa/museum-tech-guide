Millumin is a French media server application for macOS focused on simplicity, accessibility, and tight integration with the creative tools designers already use. It is widely used in theatre, contemporary dance, gallery installations, and live performance — contexts where the operator may not be a specialist AV technician.

## What Makes It Different

Millumin's dashboard-based workflow is genuinely easy to learn. Content is organised into columns and layers, warping and blending is handled visually, and it receives video from other applications in real time via Syphon. For small to medium museum installations where the team needs to operate and update content without specialist support, Millumin is often the right choice.

It also has strong OSC support, making it easy to trigger content from interactive systems, show control, or even a mobile device.

## Use Cases

- Gallery installations where the museum team operates and updates content independently
- Theatre and performance shows combining pre-rendered video with live camera feeds
- Multi-projector setups requiring quick warping and blending without a full media server workflow
- Interactive installations where content is triggered via OSC from TouchDesigner or a custom system

## Museum Relevance

The most accessible professional media server for mixed-technical teams. A good choice for installations where curators or producers need to update content without calling in a technician.
