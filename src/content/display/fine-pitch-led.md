Fine pitch LED walls are modular indoor LED displays built from panels with pixel pitches of P2.5mm or smaller — the number indicates millimetres between pixel centres. At P1.5–P2.5, viewing distances of 2–5 metres produce a seamless, high-resolution image with no visible pixels. These walls are increasingly replacing projection in museums and galleries where controlled lighting, viewing angles, or installation constraints make projectors impractical.

## Pixel Pitch and Viewing Distance

The minimum comfortable viewing distance is roughly pitch × 1000mm. So:
- **P1.5:** Comfortable from ~1.5m — suitable for kiosk-scale or close-viewing installations
- **P2.5:** Comfortable from ~2.5m — standard for gallery video walls and reception displays
- **P4–P6:** Outdoor or large-format indoor walls, comfortable from 4–6m+

Choosing too coarse a pitch for the intended viewing distance produces a visible pixel grid. Choosing too fine wastes money — the difference between P1.5 and P2.5 is invisible from 4 metres.

## LED Wall vs Projection

| | Fine Pitch LED | Projection |
|---|---|---|
| Ambient light | Works in full light | Needs controlled/dark space |
| Black levels | Deep black (off pixels) | Grey black (light scatter) |
| Brightness | 600–1500 nits | 100–300 nits typical |
| Setup | Permanent mount, complex | Flexible placement |
| Maintenance | Individual panel swap | Lamp/laser replacement |
| Cost | High upfront | Lower upfront |

## Key Manufacturers

- **Absen:** Strong in museum and broadcast. Good quality/price
- **ROE Visual:** High-end, widely used in broadcast and virtual production
- **Unilumin:** Broad range from fine pitch to outdoor
- **Samsung / LG:** Premium off-the-shelf LED wall products with integrated processors

## Signal and Control

Fine pitch LED walls require a video processor (Novastar, Brompton, Colorlight) to:
- Receive HDMI/DP/NDI/DVI inputs
- Handle colour calibration across panels
- Map the input signal to the physical panel array
- Manage brightness uniformity

## Use Cases

- High-brightness gallery display where projection cannot compete with ambient light levels
- Reception and wayfinding displays in museum entrance areas
- Immersive LED volume backgrounds for photography and content creation
- Large-format permanent installations in civic and heritage spaces
