Disguise (formerly d3 Technologies) is the industry-standard media server platform for large-scale live productions, touring shows, broadcast studios, and extended reality (XR) stages. It is the dominant choice wherever an LED wall needs to be driven at scale with precise mapping, real-time rendering, and deep integration with the rest of a production's technical stack.

## What Makes It Different

Disguise's core strength is its 3D stage model: you build a virtual replica of your physical space — screens, LED walls, projection surfaces — and map content to it in 3D. This makes complex multi-surface shows manageable and allows the same show file to be previewed virtually before the rig is built.

It also natively integrates with Notch for real-time rendered effects, and with Unreal Engine for virtual production stages.

## Use Cases

- Driving large-scale LED video walls in permanent gallery installations
- XR and virtual production stages where LED wall content must align with a camera frustum
- Complex multi-surface shows combining projection, LED, and generative content
- High-end touring and broadcast where the show must run identically night after night

## Museum Relevance

Overkill for most day-to-day museum work, but worth knowing when specifying large permanent LED installations or when working with external production companies who will bring a Disguise system into the space.
