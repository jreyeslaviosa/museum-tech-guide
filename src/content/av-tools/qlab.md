QLab (by Figure 53) is the industry standard cue playback system for theatre, live events, and museum installations on macOS. It runs sequences of audio, video, MIDI, OSC, and lighting cues from a single timeline — the way a stage manager calls a show, but automated. Almost every theatre and permanent museum installation that needs reliable, repeatable show playback uses QLab.

## How It Works

A QLab show file is a list of cues. Each cue plays back a piece of media, sends an OSC or MIDI command, fires a lighting state, or triggers another cue. Cues can be triggered manually (spacebar), by timecode, by an external OSC/MIDI message, or automatically after the previous cue.

## Use Cases

- Playing back surround sound audio for gallery installations on a schedule
- Triggering video playback, lighting states, and show control commands in sequence for live events
- Running an automated daily show that starts and stops at set times
- Receiving sensor triggers from TouchDesigner via OSC to fire content cues

## Pricing

QLab uses a licence model — the free version is functional but limits audio to stereo and video to a watermark. For installation use, a video licence (~$400) or audio licence (~$200) is typical.
