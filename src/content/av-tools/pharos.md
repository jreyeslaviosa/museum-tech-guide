Pharos Controls makes dedicated lighting control systems designed for permanent architectural and museum installations — not live events. Where GrandMA and ETC Eos are built for human operators running live shows, a Pharos controller runs unattended 24/7, triggered by schedules, calendars, external events, and show control signals. It is the standard choice when a building's lighting needs to respond to opening hours, sunrise/sunset, visitor presence, and remote management without a human operator.

## Why Pharos for Museums

Museum lighting has fundamentally different requirements from live events:
- Runs continuously without operator intervention
- Follows daily/weekly schedules and public holiday calendars
- Integrates with building management systems (BMS)
- Needs remote monitoring and fault alerts
- Gracefully handles power cycles and network drops

Pharos controllers manage all of this in firmware — they are not dependent on a connected PC staying awake.

## Key Products

- **TPC (Touch Panel Controller):** Wall-mounted touchscreen for staff control of scenes
- **LPC (Lighting Playback Controller):** Standalone controller with ArtNet/sACN/DMX output
- **VLC (Video Lighting Controller):** Adds video-synchronised lighting to media content

## Designer Software

Pharos Designer is the programming environment — drag-and-drop timeline, trigger editor, and scene library. Projects are compiled and uploaded to the controller, which then runs independently. Triggers include: time-of-day, calendar date, external contact closure, HTTP API, OSC, serial, and show control protocols.

## Use Cases

- Museum ambient lighting that transitions at opening, changes through the day, and dims at closing — automatically
- Exhibition lighting scenes triggered by visitor presence (PIR input to Pharos)
- Synchronising architectural LED with a specific media server timeline
- Remote monitoring of lighting state across multiple gallery spaces
