LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) uses pulsed laser light to measure distances and build 3D point clouds of an environment. In interactive art and installation contexts, LiDAR sensors detect the presence and position of visitors with high precision at long range, regardless of lighting conditions or body visibility.

## Common Sensors for Art Installations

- **Ouster OS series:** High-resolution 3D spinning LiDAR; wide range; outputs point cloud data
- **SICK TiM series:** 2D scanning LiDAR, popular for people tracking in large galleries (detects feet/legs)
- **Slamtec RPLIDAR:** Low-cost 2D LiDAR; good for prototyping proximity and presence detection
- **Hokuyo URG series:** Precision 2D LiDAR widely used in interactive installation research

## Use Cases

- Detecting visitor presence and position across a large gallery floor without cameras
- Triggering content zones as visitors move through space
- Building interactive installations that respond to the number and density of people
- Spatial mapping for projection mapping calibration and alignment
