Blender is the leading open-source 3D creation suite — modelling, rigging, animation, rendering, compositing, and video editing in one free application. It is used across game development, film VFX, and increasingly in museum and installation contexts for creating 3D assets, pre-rendered content, and real-time environments via its EEVEE renderer.

## Why Blender for Museum Work

Blender is the most accessible entry point to professional 3D — free, cross-platform, and with a thriving community producing tutorials and assets. For a museum tech team it fills the gap between drawing/2D tools and full game engines: creating 3D content for projection mapping, animating exhibition graphics, or building assets imported into Unreal Engine or Unity.

## Renderers

- **EEVEE:** Real-time rasterisation renderer. Fast, interactive, suitable for real-time preview and animation output. Quality has improved dramatically in Blender 4.x
- **Cycles:** Path-tracing renderer for photorealistic output. Slow but physically accurate. Good for pre-rendered exhibition content where quality matters and time is available
- **EEVEE Next (Blender 4.2+):** Dramatically improved real-time renderer, closing the gap with Cycles for many use cases

## Geometry Nodes

Blender's Geometry Nodes system is a node-based procedural modelling tool — it allows complex 3D forms to be generated algorithmically rather than manually modelled. For generative and data-driven installation content, Geometry Nodes can produce animated 3D forms driven by data or parameters.

## Grease Pencil

Blender's Grease Pencil system allows 2D illustration and animation within a 3D scene — used for explainer animations, exhibition graphics, and mixed 2D/3D content.

## Use Cases

- Creating 3D assets (objects, environments, characters) for Unreal Engine or Unity installations
- Pre-rendering animated content for projection mapping and large-scale display
- Procedural generative animation driven by Geometry Nodes for exhibition content
- Architectural visualisation renders for exhibition design and client presentations
